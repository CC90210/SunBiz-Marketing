# Content Engine Skill

Turn one idea into strong, platform-native content instead of posting the same thing everywhere.

## When to Activate
- Writing X posts or threads.
- Drafting LinkedIn posts or launch updates.
- Scripting short-form video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) or YouTube explainers.
- Repurposing articles, podcasts, demos, or docs into social content.
- Building a lightweight content plan around a launch, milestone, or theme.

## First Questions
Clarify:
- Source asset: what are we adapting from?
- Audience: builders, investors, customers, operators, or general audience?
- Platform: X, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, newsletter, or multi-platform?
- Goal: awareness, conversion, recruiting, authority, launch support, or engagement?

## Core Rules
- Adapt for the platform. Do NOT cross-post the same copy.
- Hooks matter more than summaries.
- Every post should carry one clear idea.
- Use specifics over slogans.
- Keep the ask small and clear.

## Platform Guidance

### X
- Open fast with a strong hook.
- One idea per post or per tweet in a thread.
- Keep links out of the main body (reply with links).
- Avoid hashtag spam.

### LinkedIn
- Strong first line (interrupt the scroll).
- Short, punchy paragraphs.
- Explicit framing around lessons, results, and takeaways.

### TikTok / Short Video
- First 3 seconds must interrupt attention (visual/audio hook).
- Script around visuals, not just narration.
- One demo, one claim, one CTA.

### YouTube
- Show the result/outcome early.
- Structure by chapter.
- Refresh the visual/vibe every 20-30 seconds.

## Repurposing Flow
Default cascade:
1. Anchor asset: article, video, demo, memo, or launch doc.
2. Extract 3-7 atomic ideas.
3. Write platform-native variants.
4. Trim repetition across outputs.
5. Align CTAs with platform intent.

## Deliverables
When asked for a campaign, return:
- The core angle.
- Platform-specific drafts.
- Optional posting order.
- Optional CTA variants.
- Any missing inputs needed before publishing.

## Quality Gate
Before delivering:
- Each draft reads natively for its platform.
- Hooks are strong and specific.
- No generic hype language ("game-changer", "revolutionary").
- No duplicated copy across platforms unless requested.
- The CTA matches the content and audience.

## CC's Voice Calibration Rules

Every piece of content must pass these tests before it ships:

**Tone checks:**
- [ ] Reads like a conversation at 2am with a smart friend — not a LinkedIn influencer
- [ ] Makes one specific claim — not a generic observation
- [ ] Has at least one number, date, or concrete detail (specificity beats vague)
- [ ] No hustle-culture language ("grind", "level up", "crush it", "beast mode")
- [ ] No preachy language — never tell people what they "should" do
- [ ] The word "I" appears more than "you" — this is CC's story, not a lecture
- [ ] Could close with "Only good things from now on." and it would feel earned

**Voice signature phrases (use sparingly — 1 per post max):**
- "I'm figuring this out in public."
- "Honest answer:"
- "Nobody talks about this part:"
- "Here's what actually happened:"

**Voice anti-patterns (never use):**
- "Game-changer"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Unlock the power of"
- "Transform your [anything]"
- "This is why most people fail"
- Bullet lists that start with verbs as the hook (it's slop)

## Platform-Specific Optimization Matrix

### X (Twitter) — 280 character hooks

**Format rules:**
- Hook: 240–280 characters max (leave room for reactions)
- One idea per tweet — if it needs two sentences, it needs a thread
- No hashtags in body — add 1 max in reply if at all
- No links in the tweet — add in first reply
- Best performing format: bold observation + one-word line break + evidence

**Hook templates for CC (pick and adapt):**
```
"Nobody tells you that [counterintuitive truth about entrepreneurship/AI]."
"I made $[X] doing [thing]. Here's what that actually felt like."
"Honest question: [question that challenges a common assumption]"
"[Specific outcome]. No ads. No cold email. Just [simple thing]."
"I was wrong about [thing]. Here's what changed my mind:"
```

**Character budget guide:**
- Hook: 200 chars
- Supporting detail: 60–70 chars
- Total: ≤280 (no truncation on mobile)

### LinkedIn — Authority Stories

**Format rules:**
- First line: scroll-stopping statement or vulnerable admission — no "Excited to share"
- Paragraphs: max 2 sentences each (mobile-first)
- Length: 150–300 words optimal (algorithm favors native long-form over short)
- End with a question or observation — not a CTA for clicks
- 3–5 hashtags in first comment, not in body

**Story structure (for authority posts):**
```
Line 1: [Bold observation or result]

[2-sentence context — what was happening]

[2-sentence turning point — what changed or what you did]

[2-sentence result — specific and honest, including what still didn't work]

[1-sentence takeaway framed as a question or an invitation to think]
```

**Example in CC's voice:**
```
I made $3,300 last month and barely left my apartment.

It's not because I worked harder. It's because I finally stopped doing work
that a system could do for me.

The first automation took 4 hours to build. Now it runs 50 times a day
without me. The hardest part wasn't the tech — it was trusting it enough
to let go.

What would you do with 3 hours back every day?
```

### Instagram — Carousel Templates

**Carousel structure (7–10 slides):**
```
Slide 1 (hook): Bold single claim — make them want to swipe
Slide 2 (setup): The situation or problem (relate to them)
Slide 3–6 (content): One idea per slide. Short sentences. Big fonts.
Slide 7 (pivot): The counterintuitive truth or reframe
Slide 8 (CTA): One action — follow, share, save, or DM
```

**Caption format:**
- First line mirrors Slide 1 hook
- Body: 3–4 short paragraphs (story context)
- Line break before CTA
- 8–15 hashtags at end (niche-specific, not generic)
- Max 2,200 characters — use ~600–800 for optimal reach

**Caption length guideline:** 600–900 characters for engagement posts; 1,200–2,200 for educational.

### TikTok — Pattern Interrupts

**Hook (first 3 seconds — this is everything):**
```
Pattern: [Unexpected action or statement] + [Reason to keep watching]

Examples:
"I accidentally built a $3,000/month income at 22. Here's the dumb way I did it."
"Stop doing [common thing]. I'll show you what replaced it in 60 seconds."
"POV: You're watching an AI run your business while you sleep. Let me explain."
```

**Script structure (60 seconds):**
```
0:00–0:03 → Hook (the interrupt)
0:03–0:10 → Setup (what this is about — don't slow down)
0:10–0:45 → Payoff (the actual content — specific, visual, fast)
0:45–0:55 → Reframe or punchline
0:55–1:00 → CTA ("Follow for Part 2" or "Comment [word] and I'll send you X")
```

**TikTok-specific rules:**
- Speak to camera — not a voiceover over slides
- Cut every 3–5 seconds minimum (no long static shots)
- Text overlay should reinforce audio, not replace it
- Trending audio adds ~15–25% organic reach boost — check trends weekly

## Content Calendar — 7-Day Rolling Schedule

Run this template each Monday. Fill the next 7 days. Batch-produce, schedule via Zernio.

```markdown
## Content Week — [YYYY-MM-DD to YYYY-MM-DD]

### Monday
- X: [Hook from "Honest answer:" template]
- LinkedIn: [Authority story from the week's biggest insight]

### Tuesday
- TikTok: [Pattern interrupt — process or result reveal]
- IG Story: [Behind-the-scenes of whatever is being built]

### Wednesday
- X: [Counterintuitive observation about AI or entrepreneurship]
- LinkedIn: [Share someone else's insight with CC's take added]

### Thursday
- TikTok: [Educational — "how I [did X] in [short time]"]
- IG Carousel: [Visual breakdown of a concept or result]

### Friday
- X: [End-of-week reflection — honest, specific, not hustle-porn]
- LinkedIn: [Win or miss from the week — vulnerable but professional]

### Saturday
- IG Story: [Personal — gym, music, city life — humanizing content]

### Sunday (optional — lighter)
- X: [Forward-looking thought or question]

### Notes for this week
- Theme/angle: [single topic thread running through the week]
- Content to produce Monday morning: [list assets needed]
- Posts to recycle/repurpose: [any high performers from 90+ days ago]
```

**Scheduling rules:**
- Never post more than 2 pieces of content per platform per day
- Space posts minimum 4 hours apart on same platform
- Zernio free plan limit: 20 posts/month — prioritize the 20 highest-value slots

## Engagement Metric Targets by Platform

| Platform | Target Reach | Target Engagement Rate | Target per Post |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|----------------|
| X | 500–5,000 impressions/post | >3% (likes + replies + RTs ÷ impressions) | 15+ engagements |
| LinkedIn | 1,000–10,000 impressions/post | >5% | 50+ reactions |
| Instagram | 500–3,000 reach/post | >4% | 40+ likes |
| TikTok | 1,000–20,000 views | >6% (likes ÷ views) | 60+ likes |

**When a post underperforms (below 50% of target):**
1. Check the hook — was it specific enough?
2. Check the timing — was it posted at peak hours? (Typically 7–9am or 6–9pm local)
3. Check the format — did it look native to the platform?
4. Do NOT repost — analyze and apply the learning to the next post.

**When a post overperforms (2x+ target):**
1. Log the hook, format, and topic in a "winners" file
2. Repurpose for other platforms within 48 hours (while momentum is live)
3. Consider a follow-up post on the same thread/angle

## Repurposing Workflow — 1 Long-Form → 5 Micro Pieces

Whenever CC produces a long-form asset (video, podcast, deep post, case study), run this workflow:

```
Source asset: [Long-form video / podcast episode / newsletter / case study]

Step 1 — Extract 5–7 atomic ideas (each stands alone, each is specific)
  Idea 1: [Single insight — no framing needed]
  Idea 2: [Single result or data point]
  Idea 3: [Counterintuitive observation]
  ...

Step 2 — Assign each idea to a platform format
  Idea 1 → X thread (3–5 tweets)
  Idea 2 → LinkedIn story post (150–250 words)
  Idea 3 → TikTok hook (60-second script outline)
  Idea 4 → IG carousel (7-slide structure)
  Idea 5 → X single tweet (one-liner)

Step 3 — Produce platform-native drafts
  Rule: Never copy-paste the same text across platforms.
  Rewrite each for platform grammar and audience expectation.

Step 4 — Schedule across 5–7 days
  Space them out. One idea per day. Let each breathe.

Step 5 — Archive source asset and all derivatives
  Log in memory/content-strategy.md under the relevant week.
```

**Time budget:** This workflow should take 30–45 minutes total per long-form asset when running smoothly. If it takes longer, the source asset wasn't specific enough.
