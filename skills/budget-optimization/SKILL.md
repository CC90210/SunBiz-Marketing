# SKILL: Budget Optimization

> Strategic budget allocation and pacing across platforms and campaigns.

---

## Budget Allocation Framework

### Initial Split (No Data Yet)
- Google Ads: 50% | Meta Ads: 50%
- Reasoning: Test both platforms equally, then shift based on data

### Data-Driven Reallocation (After 2+ Weeks)
- Calculate CPL per platform
- Shift budget: 60-70% to lower-CPL platform, 30-40% to higher
- Never go below 30% on any platform (maintain presence for data)

### Within-Platform Allocation
- Rank campaigns by CPL (lowest = best)
- Top performer: 40-50% of platform budget
- Middle performers: 30-40%
- New/testing campaigns: 10-20%

## Budget Pacing

### Daily Budget Rules
- **Underspending (<80% of daily budget):** Targeting too narrow OR bid too low
- **Perfect pacing (80-100%):** Healthy delivery
- **Overspending (>100%):** Platform may spend up to 2x daily budget (averages over month)

## Scaling Rules
1. **Never increase budget by more than 20% at once** — Sudden budget jumps reset Meta's learning phase
2. **Scale every 3-4 days** if performance is stable
3. **If CPL increases after scaling** — Wait 3 days for algorithm to stabilize
4. **If CPL still high after 5 days** — Revert to previous budget

## Budget Minimums
| Platform | Campaign Type | Recommended Minimum |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| Google Search | Lead Gen | $20/day |
| Google Display | Awareness | $15/day |
| Meta Lead Gen | Lead Generation | $20/day |
| Meta Awareness | Brand Awareness | $10/day |

## Emergency Budget Actions
| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Budget exhausted early in day | Reduce bid, widen targeting, or increase budget |
| Campaign burning cash with no leads | Pause immediately, investigate |
| End of month, budget remaining | Increase daily budget on best performers |
| Client requests budget cut | Pause worst performers first, keep best |
