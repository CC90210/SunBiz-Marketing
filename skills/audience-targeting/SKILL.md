# SKILL: Audience Targeting

> Strategic audience building for MCA campaigns across Google and Meta — optimized for CREDIT category restrictions.

---

## The CREDIT Category Reality

Under Meta's Special Ad Category (CREDIT), traditional demographic targeting is severely restricted. The winning approach for MCA ads:

1. **Broad geographic targeting** + let the algorithm learn from conversions
2. **Ad copy self-qualification** — messaging filters the audience naturally
3. **Data-modeled audiences** — train the pixel from funded deal patterns
4. **Custom conversions with "Other" event** — avoids algorithmic demographic bias

---

## Targeting Strategy: Funnel-Based

### Prospecting (Cold — 60% of Budget)
- **Google:** Search campaigns with high-intent MCA keywords
- **Meta:** Broad national, business owner interests, CREDIT-compliant
- **Message:** Consolidation before/after, educational content
- **Goal:** Drive qualified JotForm submissions

### Retargeting (Warm — 25% of Budget)
- **Meta:** JotForm visitors who didn't submit, video viewers (50%+), page engagers
- **Google:** RLSA (remarketing lists) on MCA search terms
- **Message:** Case studies, urgency, "Your consolidation plan is waiting"
- **Goal:** Convert engaged prospects

### High-Intent (Hot — 15% of Budget)
- **Google:** Exact match MCA consolidation keywords
- **Meta:** Form abandoners, multiple page visits
- **Message:** "Complete your application" / "Talk to a specialist"
- **Goal:** Close warm leads

## Meta Ads Targeting (CREDIT Restrictions)

### What You CAN Target
- **Location:** Country, state, city, DMA (15-mile minimum radius)
- **Interests:** Small business, entrepreneurship, business finance, restaurant owner, contractor, retail business, construction
- **Behaviors:** Business page admins, small business owners, engaged shoppers
- **Connections:** People who like SunBiz Funding Page

### What You CANNOT Target (CREDIT Category)
- Age ranges (must be 18-65+)
- Gender (must be all)
- Zip codes (minimum 15-mile radius)
- Multicultural affinity
- Lookalike audiences

### Self-Qualification via Ad Copy
Since we can't target demographics, use copy to filter:
- "If your business does $15K+/month and you're paying back more than one advance..."
- "Business owners with 2+ MCA positions — see your consolidation options"
- Revenue thresholds and position counts naturally filter unqualified traffic

### Best Practice: Broad + Algorithm
1. Start with broad geographic targeting (national)
2. Add a few relevant interests (small business, entrepreneurship)
3. Let Meta's algorithm find the right people from conversion signals
4. Use custom conversions with "Other" event to avoid demographic bias
5. Over time, upload funded deal data to train the pixel further

### Custom Audiences
| Type | Source | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| Website | Meta Pixel (JotForm visitors) | Retargeting form visitors |
| Form Abandoners | Started form but didn't submit | High-intent retargeting |
| Customer List | Funded deal CRM export (hashed) | Data-modeled signals |
| Engagement | Page/post interactions | Warm audience |
| Video | Video ad viewers (50%+ watched) | Video retargeting |

### Data-Modeled Audiences (Advanced)
Build behavioral models from funded deal data:
1. Export funded merchant data (hashed emails, phone numbers)
2. Upload as custom audience to Meta
3. Meta learns conversion patterns from these users
4. Algorithm finds similar behavioral profiles without explicit targeting
5. Refresh monthly with new funded deal data

## Audience Testing Framework

1. Create 3 audience segments:
   - **Broad National** (minimal interests, algorithm-driven)
   - **Interest-Based** (small business + entrepreneurship + industry interests)
   - **Data-Modeled** (custom audience from funded deals, once available)
2. Run all 3 with same budget and creative
3. After 7 days + 50 conversions, compare CPQL (not just CPL)
4. Shift budget to winner, pause worst performer
5. Iterate with refined messaging on winning audience
